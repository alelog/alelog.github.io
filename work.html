I am the Vice President of Research at GrammaTech, a leading cyber-security technology company. At GrammaTech, I oversee both technical and business aspects of the Research Division.

My research work at GrammaTech has focused on binary-analysis technologies, including both formal and heuristic techniques. A key contribution of my early work at GrammaTech was the addition of Binary-Analysis Capabilities to CodeSonar. In recent years, work in my division began to explore statistical, machine-learning, and evolutionary-computation techniques in an effort to address the challenges of scalability and precision in program analysis and repair.

I received a Ph.D. in Computer Sciences from the University of Wisconsin, where my thesis exposed a new connection between machine learning and program analysis. Before joining GrammaTech, I worked at Hewlett-Packard and the IBM T.J. Watson Research Center.
